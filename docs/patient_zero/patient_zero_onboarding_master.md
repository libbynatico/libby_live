## Advocacy Principles

1. **State-first, not chat-first.** Every statement should be sourced or labelled Draft/Inferred.
2. **Attrition before recognition.** Frame delay as the systemic pattern, not exception.
3. **Functional, not clinical.** When clinical records are unavailable, argue functional impact (school records, ADL limitations, reliance on third-party support).
4. **Administrative barriers as evidence.** 32 call attempts = documented failure of system accommodation.
5. **Confidence labels on everything.** Confirmed | Inferred | Missing Evidence — never let a caseworker or adjudicator conflate them.
6. **Nonverbal mode is an accommodation.** Libby's briefing console is not a productivity tool — it is an access tool.
