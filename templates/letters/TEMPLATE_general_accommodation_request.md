# TEMPLATE: General AODA Accommodation Request
# Use for: Any organization, government office, clinic, pharmacy, employer, housing provider, etc.
# Legal basis: AODA 2005 + Ontario Human Rights Code

---

## TEMPLATE: One-Page Accommodation Request Letter

```
TO: [Organization / Person Name]
[Address / Department]

FROM: Matthew Herbert
Email: mattherbert01@gmail.com
DATE: [Today's Date]

RE: Accommodation Request Under AODA and Ontario Human Rights Code

---

Dear [Name / Organization],

I am writing to formally request accommodation for the following disabilities
under the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA), 2005, and
the Ontario Human Rights Code.

MY DISABILITIES:
1. Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI) — onset 2001, age 12
   Post-concussive: memory disruption, auditory processing deficits, photophobia,
   cognitive fatigue, executive dysfunction.

2. Crohn's Disease — diagnosed 2002
   With permanent ileostomy (since 2015); fatigue cycles; unpredictable medical
   needs may affect availability.

I am an ODSP recipient (approved December 2025), confirming the substantial
and ongoing nature of these disabilities.

ACCOMMODATIONS I REQUIRE:

COMMUNICATION:
[ ] All information provided in writing (not verbal-only)
[ ] Written summaries after meetings
[ ] Email as primary communication channel (not phone)
[ ] Plain language — no unexplained jargon
[ ] One topic at a time; segmented communication

ENVIRONMENT:
[ ] Reduced lighting when possible (photophobia)
[ ] Reduced noise / quiet room
[ ] Extended time for appointments and processing

PROCESS:
[ ] Allow third-party support person to be present
[ ] Extra time for decision-making — no same-day deadlines on complex matters
[ ] Appointment confirmation in writing 48 hours before
[ ] Flexibility for rescheduling due to fatigue or medical condition
[ ] Allow written responses in place of phone calls

SPECIFIC TO THIS INTERACTION:
[Add specific request here — e.g., "I require written instructions for the
application process" or "I need a quiet appointment space" or "I need extra
time to complete the form."]

YOUR OBLIGATIONS:
Under the AODA (2005) and Ontario Human Rights Code, you are required to
provide reasonable accommodation to the point of undue hardship. If you
believe you cannot provide any of the accommodations above, please advise
in writing with the specific reason, and I will work with you to find
alternatives.

If you have questions about AODA obligations, please contact your
organization's AODA coordinator.

Thank you for your attention to this request.

Respectfully,

Matthew Herbert
mattherbert01@gmail.com
[Address]
[Date]

OHRC: 1-800-387-9080 | AODA Info: aodainfo@ontario.ca
```
