Why These Accommodations Exist — TBI Impact Since Age 12
Memory
Verbal information is not reliably retained. Written backup is not a preference — it is how information is processed.
Processing Speed
Multi-step or dense information causes cognitive overload. One topic at a time allows accurate comprehension.
Sensory Sensitivity
Photophobia and auditory sensitivity are present daily. Bright environments and loud spaces reduce functional capacity.
Fatigue
Cognitive fatigue may limit appointment availability. Appointments after 2pm or during medical recovery periods may need to be rescheduled.
Phone Communication
Phone conversations are unreliable — both due to hardware instability and TBI processing barriers. Email or written communication is preferred and more accurate.
Complex Systems
Navigating multi-step government or administrative processes independently is not possible without written step-by-step guidance and third-party support.
Communication Protocol
Please Do
  • Provide written summaries after all meetings
  • Use plain language — avoid jargon and acronyms
  • State one topic or instruction at a time
  • Confirm understanding before moving on
  • Allow written responses instead of phone calls
  • Give 48-hour written appointment confirmation
  • Allow a support person to be present
  • Use email as primary communication channel
Please Do Not
  • Require phone-only communication
  • Present multiple topics in a single conversation
  • Assume comprehension from a nod or "yes"
  • Require same-day decisions on complex matters
  • Interpret rescheduling as non-compliance
  • Use jargon without plain-language explanation
  • Conduct meetings in loud or bright environments
  • Deny support person access during appointments
Appointment Planning
For All Appointments
Allow extra time. Provide all appointment details in writing. Confirm 48 hours before via email or text. I may need to reschedule due to fatigue or medical condition — this is not non-compliance, it is the disability.
